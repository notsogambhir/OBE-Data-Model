NBA OBE Portal: Comprehensive Attainment Logic Guide
This guide details the mathematical framework used to transition from raw student performance to Program Outcome (PO) achievement levels.
Example 1: Single Student, Many-to-Many Mapping & Attempt Logic
Scenario: A student takes an assessment where questions are mapped to multiple COs. Unattempted questions are excluded from the “Maximum Obtainable” denominator for that specific student.
Data Setup:
Course Target: 50%
Question 1 (CO1): Max 10, Obtained 8
Question 2 (CO1 & CO2): Max 20, Unattempted
Question 3 (CO2): Max 10, Obtained 7
Mathematics:
CO1 Calculation:
Questions involved: Q1, Q2.
Because Q2 is unattempted, it is ignored in both numerator and denominator.
Max Obtainable: 10 (Q1)
Attained Marks: 8 (Q1)
CO1 Percentage: (8 / 10) * 100 = 80%
Result: Attained (80% >= 50%)
CO2 Calculation:
Questions involved: Q2, Q3.
Max Obtainable: 10 (Q3)
Attained Marks: 7 (Q3)
CO2 Percentage: (7 / 10) * 100 = 70%
Result: Attained (70% >= 50%)

Example 4: Complex End-to-End Workflow (M:M & Attempt Logic)
Process Description:
Question Level: Every question is tagged to one or more COs.
Student Attempt Filter: For Student X, if Question Y is ‘U’ (Unattempted), Question Y is removed from the Max Marks calculation for all COs it was mapped to.
CO Student %: For every CO, sum the obtained marks and divide by the student-specific adjusted max marks.
Binary Mapping: If Student CO % >= Course Target, the student is “Successful” (1), else (0).
CO Attainment Level: Calculate % of successful students in the section. Map this % to Level 1, 2, or 3 based on course-specific thresholds.
